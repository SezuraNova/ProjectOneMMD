The Anti-Twitter Project

Social Media and the Problem with Bad Incentives

The Attention Economy

Social platforms influence how we interact with politics. Stories, viewpoints and figures are promoted algorithmically based not on their relevance, veracity or importance; but by the amount of time a user spends looking, this is the Attention Economy. how much attention a post or tweet gets can be broken down in a variety of ways: something can be novel, funny or informative; noticeably however it can also be depressing, terrifying or even infuriating. Now when all of these are equally incentivised by the ad-based model what are the results of such a model.

The Political Problem

These issues arise due to how we've chosen to structure our social platforms. Algorithmically building our news feed by what alligns to our views and filtering out those that don't, This is known as the Filter Bubble. Moreover when opposing views are introduced, the algorithm is incentivised to introduce such elements in the most inflammatory way possible, here outrage becomes synonymous with attention.
"Social media rewards outrage, false facts, and filter bubbles – which are better at capturing attention – and divides us so we can no longer agree on truth." - Tristan Harris
This is problematic not only because the incentive is to keep the user on site, within the loop of outrage and not acting on their political or moral motivations outside of the digital landscape. It also has the potential to drive user further towards their current political predilections and reinforce an echo-chamber style of political engagement. This in turn can lead to false information, that confirms their narrative, being preferred and thus algorithmically promoted. This can exacerbates the phenomena of "fake news" and propaganda.

Mobile Media Based Solutions

Solution 1 Political Cash-out: An avenue out of cycle of outrage.

Social Media Platforms can increase political engagement. However it's important as a society to take this political energy into the real world and enact change, as opposed to it just being monetized for ad revenue. Thus the Cash-out app would profile the user's political views and show them real world organisations that align with those values. These Political profile can be constructed with a mixture of user instigated political quizzes and Natural Language Processing scraping the user’s social media profiles for politically associated keywords. The Organisation profiles on the other hand are constructed based on their respective website information, nearest location, independent profile(eg. wikipedia) and contact details; moreover these organisation profiles would be subject to human moderation. To illustrate a user experience of Cash-out, someone who repeatedly shares or retweets pro-veganism, animal rights or cruelty-free farming posts or tweets; and through their own answers to questionnaires Cash-out recognizes them as having pro animal stance. The user can then be shown groups such as the World Wildlife Fund, SAFE For Animals or the SPCA, given a breakdown about the organisation and links to volunteer work, Job opportunities, donation portals, upcoming protests or demonstrations, and any other ways the organisation in question wishes to be supported.

Solution 2 Common Ground: Encouraging political cohesion and empathy

In an attempt to address both the Echo chamber and Political Tribalism this combined plugin and App would construct a newsfeed covering political content that represents common ground across different political stances. The plugin would allow the user to politically profile themselves before engaging with social media. Then as they post and tweet, they can via the plugin also rate their level of agreement with content they engage with and tag said content. Then through the dedicated App's newsfeed posts and tweets will be promoted based not just on relevance and interest, but also on agreement and disagreement across the political spectrum, meaning that content with apolitical consensus will be preferred. The newsfeed will pull directly from the social media platform the user specifies and restructure the content feed with the new consensus value in mind promoting content with high-levels of consensus but also taking into account the default weighting of content specified by the platform. Moreover the App will allow the user to see newsfeeds based on specific political profiles, giving them an escape from within their own filter bubble and allowing them to see content from other perspectives. This is done by aggregating the public content(eg. News Sites) from user’s of similar political profiles into a singular public newsfeed representative of the group. The user generated tags system mentioned above will allow users to search for content of specific types to more fine tune their experience. Moreover it will also be statistically relevant in recognizing points where opinions across different political groups coalesce or divide.
